Toronto stocks boosted by bond party and strong banking and gold sectors 

The Toronto Stock Exchange's main index rose on Wednesday, boosted by strong gains in the banking and gold sectors. The S&P/TSX composite index closed up 114.99 points, or 0.7%, at 16,383.63.

The financials sector gained 0.9% as bond yields rose. Higher bond yields can boost the margin that banks make between lending and borrowing costs. Royal Bank of Canada and Toronto-Dominion Bank, two of the country's biggest lenders, rose 1% and 0.7% respectively.

The materials sector, which includes precious and base metal miners and energy producers, gained 1.5% as gold prices hit their highest in more than six years. Barrick Gold Corp rose 3.5% and Eldorado Gold Corp was up 6.2%.

"The gold miners are getting a big boost today from higher gold prices... there's also a fair bit of optimism on the trade front with China and the U.S. optimistically hoping for a phase one deal before the end of the year," said Greg Taylor, chief investment officer at Purpose Investments.  

U.S. gold futures rose to $1,463.70 per ounce, the highest since May 2013. Gold tends to gain in times of economic uncertainty or geopolitical tensions. Investors kept an eye on the latest twists in the lengthy Sino-U.S. trade war. Top U.S. and Chinese negotiators spoke on the phone on Tuesday and agreed to continue discussions.

However, energy stocks fell 0.4% despite higher oil prices as several producers cut their spending plans for next year. The move could slow growth in the sector. Oil prices rose after an industry report showed a surprise drop in U.S. crude stockpiles.    

The tech sector gained ground, providing the biggest boost to the main index, rising 1.1%. Open Text Corp advanced 4.4% after the software company exceeded analysts' expectations for quarterly revenue and profit.

In economic news, Canadian wholesale trade unexpectedly fell in October, dropping 0.7% as sales of machinery, equipment and supplies declined. Analysts has forecast a 0.3% increase.
