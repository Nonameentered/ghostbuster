Dean Foods Inc. Set to Acquire in Fluid Milk Business, Expects Growth in Specialty Segment 

Dean Foods Inc., one of the nation's largest dairy processors and distributors, announced today that it has entered into an agreement to acquire Midwest Dairy Processors, a small fluid milk processor based in Wisconsin. The acquisition will expand Dean Foods' fluid milk business and allow the company to better serve customers in the Midwest region.

Midwest Dairy Processors operates two milk processing plants in Wisconsin that primarily produce half gallons and gallons of white and flavored milk. The company distributes products under its own brand and as a private label product to retailers and foodservice customers in Wisconsin, Minnesota, Iowa, and Illinois. Dean Foods expects the acquisition to add approximately $150 million in annual net sales.

"This acquisition further strengthens our fluid milk business and enables us to supply more customers and consumers in the Midwest," said Eric Beringause, President and CEO of Dean Foods. "By gaining additional processing capacity and capabilities, we are well positioned to meet increased customer and consumer demand for our products in this important region."

The $125 million cash transaction is expected to close by the end of the third quarter, subject to customary regulatory approvals. According to Beringause, Dean Foods expects the acquisition to be accretive to the company's earnings within the first 12 months following the close of the transaction.

Beyond fluid milk, Dean Foods aims to grow its specialty dairy business, which includes creamers, cultured products, and plant-based beverages. According to Dean Foods Chief Growth Officer Brad Cashaw, "We see significant opportunities to expand our portfolio of dairy products to meet changing consumer tastes and needs. Our specialty segments remain a key driver of future growth for the company."

Dean Foods is in the midst of a strategic review to evaluate various options to accelerate its business transformation, strengthen its balance sheet, and maximize value for stakeholders. Possible options include selling non-core assets, reducing costs, and improving operational efficiencies. The company expects to complete the strategic review by the end of 2019.

In 2018, Dean Foods generated $7.8 billion in net sales and $823 million in adjusted EBITDA. The company's diversified portfolio of regional and national brands and products includes DairyPure, TruMoo, Alta Dena, Berkeley Farms, Country Fresh, Dean's, Friendly's, Garelick Farms, LAND O LAKES* milk and cultured products*, Lehigh Valley Dairy Farms, Mayfield, McArthur, Meadow Gold, Oak Farms, PET, Shenandoah's Pride, Suiza, Lil' Drug Store, and many more. Dean Foods was founded in 1925 and is headquartered in Dallas, Texas.
