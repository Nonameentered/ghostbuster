Charter Plc Agrees to £378 Million Bid for Howden Group Plc, World's Largest Industrial Ventilation and Fan Supplier

Charter Plc, a leading global engineering company, announced today that it has reached an agreement to acquire Howden Group Plc for £378 million in an all-cash transaction. Howden is the world's largest designer and manufacturer of industrial fans, industrial blowers, rotary heat exchangers and dedicated custom blowers. 

Founded in 1854, Howden has a long history of innovation and currently employs over 4,300 people across 30 countries, with manufacturing facilities in the Americas, Europe, Asia, Africa, and Australia. Howden's products and services are used in a wide range of applications, including ventilation, aeration and material handling across industries such as mining, transportation, power generation, and wastewater treatment.

"Howden is a global leader with an exceptional portfolio of products and technologies for mission-critical industrial applications," said John Evans, Chief Executive of Charter. "This acquisition significantly strengthens our engineering capabilities and product range, while enabling us to penetrate new end markets. We are excited to welcome Howden's employees to the Charter family."

Under the terms of the acquisition, Charter will purchase the entire issued share capital of Howden for £378 million. The deal is expected to close in the third quarter of this year, subject to regulatory approvals and other customary closing conditions. Howden will continue operating under its own brand and will form a new Industrial Ventilation division within Charter. 

Neil Singer, Chief Executive of Howden, said, "We are very pleased to be joining Charter, which shares our values of engineering excellence, innovation and trusted partner service. Becoming part of Charter will enable us to leverage their global footprint and resources to accelerate our growth. This transaction is a strong endorsement of Howden and recognizes the dedication and skill of our employees around the world, as well as the quality of our products and services."

Charter expects the acquisition to be immediately accretive to earnings per share and generate pre-tax cost synergies of £20 million within three years. Howden has a strong track record of growth and profitability, with revenues of £612 million and EBITDA of £92 million in its fiscal year ended December 31, 2020.
