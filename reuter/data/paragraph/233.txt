"The problem is that there are many other sites on the web that have comparable weaknesses as the Justice Department web site," said Richard Power, editor of Security Focus, an online security magazine.  The hackers who wreaked havoc on the Justice Department site were not caught but experts said the alarm from this cyber crime spree should serve as a wake-up call to organisations that have relied on the false security of passwords and firewalls.  "There's no security on large parts of the Internet," said Bruce Schneier, author of "Applied Cryptography," a bible of sorts for Internet security. "People really don't understand what they need."
