The court's decision, along with proposals in Congress to change the 1974 amendment, will have a dramatic impact on the way derivatives and foreign exchange investments are regulated in the United States, securities lawyers said. "This case is really riding the crest of a wave, to use a cliche," said Ephraim Garnett, a Washington lawyer representing several Wall Street groups in the case. "Both houses of Congress are looking into the entire issue of futures and derivatives trading."
