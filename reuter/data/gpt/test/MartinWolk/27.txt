Itron Inc., a global technology and services company engaged in the energy and water industries, saw its shares plummet more than 25% on Friday, July 30, 2021, after the company issued a warning regarding its second-quarter earnings.
The company announced that its revenue for the second quarter would be approximately $2.1 billion, lower than previous guidance of $2.2 billion to $2.3 billion. Itron said that the lower revenue was primarily due to supply chain issues and the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic. The company also expects to incur additional costs associated with these issues.
Itron's stock fell 25.8% to $98.19 in heavy trading on Friday, making it one of the biggest decliners on the Nasdaq Stock Market. The stock is now down more than 30% for the year.
Analysts had been expecting Itron to report revenue of $2.27 billion in the second quarter, according to data from Refinitiv. The company is scheduled to release its second-quarter earnings results on August 2, 2021.
Following the warning, several analysts downgraded Itron's stock. Robert W. Baird downgraded the stock to "neutral" from "outperform" and lowered its price target to $110 from $145. Raymond James also downgraded the stock to "market perform" from "outperform" and reduced its price target to $100 from $135.
