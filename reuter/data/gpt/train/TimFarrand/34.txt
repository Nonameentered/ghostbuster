Tate and Lyle's CEO Larry Pillard expressed optimism regarding the rebound of the company's Staley unit. Despite tough market conditions in North America, Pillard shared in a recent interview that cost-cutting measures and a reduction in excess inventory has positioned the company for success in the near future. Additionally, innovation in product development and a focus on sustainability are expected to drive growth. Tate and Lyle is a global provider of food and beverage ingredients with a portfolio that includes sweeteners, starches, and texturants. The Staley unit primarily focuses on corn processing and serves various industries such as food, paper, and fuel.
