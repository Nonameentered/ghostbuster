Cathay Pacific Airways has announced the beginning of the construction of its new HK$1.6 billion headquarters at Chek Lap Kok. The new headquarters will comprise of two office towers linked together with a skybridge.
The construction of the state-of-the-art facility is expected to be completed in 2023 and will provide a refreshed and modern workspace for all of Cathay Pacific's teams. The building will also incorporate green and sustainable features such as energy-efficient lighting, a green wall and rainwater recycling system.
The move to a new headquarters comes as Cathay Pacific looks to streamline operations and reduce expenses in the wake of the Covid-19 pandemic. The new building will offer a significant reduction in overhead costs compared to the current headquarters located in Central.
The new facility reflects Cathay Pacific's commitment to Hong Kong and its future as a global aviation hub. The airline has maintained a continued presence in Hong Kong since its establishment in 1946 and is currently the largest airline based in the city.
