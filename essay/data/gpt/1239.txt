The neurological system is one of the most complex and essential systems in the human body. It consists of the brain, the spinal cord, and the complex network of neurons and nerves that are spread throughout the body. The neurological system is responsible for controlling and coordinating all body functions, including movement, sensation, thought, and behavior.
The central nervous system (CNS) and the peripheral nervous system (PNS) are the two major components of the neurological system. The CNS is composed of the brain and spinal cord, while the PNS includes all the nerves that branch out from the CNS and extend throughout the body. The fundamental difference between the two systems is that the CNS is responsible for processing and interpreting information, while the PNS is responsible for transmitting information to and from the CNS.
There are various brain scanning techniques available to medical professionals to help diagnose brain diseases and injuries. These methodologies include computed tomography (CT) scans, magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), positron emission tomography (PET), and electroencephalography (EEG). Each technique measures different aspects of brain activity and provides unique information about the brain's structure and function.
Brain scanning technology and other discoveries relating to the neurological system have the potential to revolutionize medical science. Future applications may include the development of new treatments for neurological disorders such as Alzheimer's disease, Parkinson's disease, and epilepsy. They may also lead to the development of better brain implants for people with paralysis and other disabilities.
Ganglia are clusters of nerve cells located in the PNS. They function to control and regulate the body's involuntary functions, such as heart rate and digestion. The ganglion is part of the autonomic nervous system and plays a critical role in controlling various functions of the body.

The CNS and PNS work together to ensure the body is functioning correctly, and several brain scanning techniques are available to help diagnose potential problems.
