What was the 'passionlessness' debate and how did it impact the gendered life of Americans, particularly women, during the nineteenth century? Analyze the ideology of 'passionlessness' and its link to the Cult of True Womanhood, as well as the role of religion, medicine, and class in shaping the debate. To what extent was 'passionlessness' liberating or repressive for women, and how did it intersect with issues of race and slavery?
