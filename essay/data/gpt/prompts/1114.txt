Is custom still a significant source of international law despite its inherent complications and uncertainties? This essay explores the role of custom in international conventions, the use of force, and the role of peremptory norms, and discusses the problems with the two elements within custom: state practice and opinio juris. It also briefly touches on the importance of 'soft law' such as United Nations Resolutions. Ultimately, the essay considers whether custom is now of limited importance as a source of international law.
