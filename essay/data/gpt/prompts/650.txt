Examine the ambiguity of the realist critique towards liberal internationalism, considering the core liberal assumption of the importance of ideas and morality in building an international society. How has the realist perspective "borrowed" from liberalism, and to what extent does this render their critique ambiguous? With reference to historical and contemporary developments in world politics, explore the underlying questions of the First Debate of International Relations, including the possibility of common interests, moral visions, cooperation, and the abolition of war.
