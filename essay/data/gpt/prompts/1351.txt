What is Battered Woman Syndrome and how does it affect the plea of provocation as a defence to murder? How does the concept of BWS challenge traditional notions of self-defence and provocation, and is it applicable to all women or based on a euro-centric notion of passive, weak, and fearful victims of domestic violence? Examine these questions in the larger context of sexual divisions in law and the structured nature of violence against women, while considering the historical and cultural experiences of women of different races, classes, and sexual orientations.
