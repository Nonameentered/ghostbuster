Before launching into the essay proper, it is perhaps necessary to consider the approach that one should take when reading the pamphlet. While Bernstein's view that Winstanley used "religious phraseology as a cloak" has been superceded by writers more sympathetic to Winstanley's religious propensities This larger dispute about Winstanley's religious phraseology is somewhat defined by their different interpretations of what Winstanley meant by being "in a trance" Their different approaches to Winstanley's religious phraseology are perhaps most amenable to their respective evaluations of Winstanley's development as a thinker.
