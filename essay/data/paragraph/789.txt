However, Beier and Finlay believe that 'the direct influence of Government on London's growth has probably been overstated' The size of the central government does not change much between 1580 and 1640 when London experienced its biggest population increase. The increase in officials came after 1650. By 1725 the number of officials had risen to around 2,700 from only 400 under Elizabeth. Nevertheless, even 2,700 officials and their family and servants would have only taken up around 'five and a half per cent'
