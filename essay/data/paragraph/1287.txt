The legal environment is comfortable for companies operating existing light rail systems, anti-competitive practices such as fixed fares or line extensions by the incumbent operators are prohibited by the Competition act (1998) and the Consumer protection Act (1987). This legal protection makes it difficult to enter the industry as a new operator.  The main criterion for deciding whether to proceed with a new venture is whether a reasonable financial return can be achieved.
